Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) Famous for: Theory of Relativity, photoelectric effect, contributions to quantum mechanics.


Key Inventions/Discoveries:
  • Theory of Special Relativity: Revolutionized the way we understand space, time, and energy.
  • E=mc²: Formula that shows the relationship between energy and mass.
  • Photoelectric Effect: This helped establish quantum theory and earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921

Thomas Alva Edison

Thomas Edison (1847–1931) Famous for:Inventions in electricity, motion pictures, and sound recording.


Key Inventions/Discoveries:
  • Light Bulb: Edison is often credited with inventing the practical incandescent light bulb, although he improved upon earlier designs.
  • Phonograph: Edison invented the phonograph in 1877, a device capable of recording and reproducing sound, considered the first true sound recording device.
  • Motion Pictures:Edison helped develop the first commercially successful motion picture camera and projector, contributing greatly to the film industry.
  • Electric Power Distribution: He created one of the first electric power stations, helping to develop the infrastructure for electric lighting.

Marie Curie

Marie Curie (1867–1934)
Famous for: Pioneering research on radioactivity, first woman to win a Nobel Prize.


Key Inventions/Discoveries:
  • Discovery of Polonium and Radium: Curie discovered two radioactive elements, greatly expanding the field of atomic physics.
  • Work on Radioactivity: She developed the theory of radioactivity and techniques for isolating radioactive isotopes.
  • First Mobile X-Ray Units: During WWI, she helped equip ambulances with X-ray machines to assist wounded soldiers.

Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943)
Famous for: AC (Alternating Current) electricity system, innovations in electromagnetism and wireless communication.


Key Inventions/Discoveries:
  • Alternating Current (AC): Developed the AC power system still used today for power transmission.
  • Tesla Coil: A high-voltage transformer used in early radio and television tech.
  • Radio Waves: Tesla worked on wireless transmission and was a pioneer of radio communication.
